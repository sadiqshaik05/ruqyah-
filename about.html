📖 WHAT IS RUQYAH SHARIAH?

Ruqyah Shariah is a spiritual healing method in Islam that involves reciting verses from the Qur’an, duas (supplications), and authentic prophetic traditions (Hadith) to cure physical illnesses, emotional distress, spiritual ailments, sihr (black magic), ayn (evil eye), and possession by jinn. It is a halal and prescribed remedy for all believers facing unseen or unexplained afflictions. Ruqyah is not superstition—it is rooted in divine revelation and prophetic practice. We strive to create a safe, halal, and trustworthy environment for those seeking relief through the words of Allah ﷻ.

🕋 Qur'anic Foundation for Ruqyah?

  • Surah Al-Isra (17:82):
    • “And We send down from the Qur'an that which is a healing and a mercy to those who believe…”
  • Surah Fussilat (41:44):
    • “…Say, it is for those who believe, a guidance and healing…”
  • Ayat al-Kursi (Surah Baqarah 2:255):
    • Considered the most powerful verse for protection and frequently recited during Ruqyah.
  • Surah Al-Falaq & Surah An-Naas:
    • These two chapters, known as Mu'awwidhatayn, were recited by the Prophet ﷺ every night for protection.

✨ Types of Ruqyah

  • Ruqyah Shariah (Permissible):
    • 1) Based on Qur’anic verses.
    2) Authentic duas from Hadith.
    3) Recited by oneself or someone righteous.
    4) Must not include shirk, magic, or innovation.
  • Ruqyah Shirkiah (Forbidden):
    • 1) Involves charms, amulets, calling jinn, or invoking other than Allah.
    2) Based on superstition, fortune-telling, or black magic.
    3) Strictly prohibited in Islam.
